Priority Recommendations for CHRO
Immediate action plan — ranked by impact
1
Fix the offer stage — close the 88.6% collapse (URGENT) 65.3% of offers are below candidate expectations by avg 7.5%. Introduce pre-offer salary alignment conversations. Compress offer-to-join window from 34 days to under 14. Losing candidates here wastes the entire pipeline cost upstream.
2
Eliminate the 34-day accept-to-join gap (URGENT) 87 candidates accepted then didn't join — likely lost to counter-offers during the waiting period. Implement structured pre-onboarding: day-1 kit, team intros, role prep within 72h of acceptance. Target: reduce gap to ≤14 days.
3
Cut the Apply→Phone lag from 11 days (URGENT) First response takes 11 days on average — top candidates accept competing offers during this window. "Slow process" is the #3 withdrawal reason (59 cases). Target: phone screen within 3 days of application.
4
Reallocate budget away from AngelList and Employee Referrals AngelList: 1.1% join rate with 16.1% withdrawal rate — worst ROI. Employee Referrals: 1.6% join rate. Indeed (4.2%) and Naukri (3.7%) deliver nearly 4x better yield. Shift 40% of budget to these channels immediately.
5
Focus on Design and Customer Support — critically understaffed Both departments joined only 5 candidates from 300+ applications (1.6–1.7% join rate). Design has the lowest offer acceptance rate (11.5%). Audit JDs, salary bands, and screening criteria for these two departments first.
6
Redesign interview scoring — current scores are not predictive Joined candidates score only marginally higher than rejected ones across all 4 interview stages (max diff: 0.38 points on culture fit out of 10). Scores are not differentiating talent. Introduce structured rubrics, calibration sessions, and blind scoring.
7
Address the "Missing Key Skills" and "Communication Concerns" rejection cluster These two reasons account for 441 rejections (28.6% of all rejections) — more than any other cause. This likely signals poor JD quality attracting under-qualified applicants, or screening criteria that are too vague. Rewrite JDs for top 5 roles with specific skill requirements and pre-screening questions.
